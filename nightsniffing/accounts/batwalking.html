Some quotes from participants

Perception

We saw something flying past and hoped it was a bat and actually it turned out it wasn't, it was a bird, usually. I think when you have a bat detector, you are far more reliant on your ears than your eyes. Especially when it's quite dark.

When you hear the clicking, there’s that initial thing of ‘ah, I got something’ but then remember to look up to the sky and not look at your clicky thing! Because that’s what I was doing, I was looking at that although that was where the bat was going to be. I had to remind myself – don’t look at that, look up at the sky.

Anxiety

Especially when it’s just one person or a couple of people watching you walk around… you feel like a bit of a… geek, for want of a better word. And they’re thinking – what the hell are you doing? And I want to explain: it’s about bats!

[The bat detector] also just amplifies the amount of stuff that's in the air. And that's not necessarily a good feeling, of knowing there is all this […] it just amplifies the fact you can't access, there are all these spectrums of information you can't access. You feel fundamentally inadequate.

Collectivity

you were urged to move your body to try and reach them, it felt a bit like you are dancing [...] it felt like everyone was throwing their arms around and running after the bat, like trying to get into the rhythm of finding them.

I also now get as much enjoyment from watching other people react to seeing bats for the first time, so there's a real sense of satisfaction [...] especially the young kids, and even adults, seeing that wonderment and oh wow they've not seen it before.
